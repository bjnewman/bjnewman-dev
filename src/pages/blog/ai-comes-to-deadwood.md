***INT. GEM SALOON - SWEARENGEN'S OFFICE - DAY***

AL SWEARENGEN stands at the balcony doors, peering down at the mud of the thoroughfare. Behind him, A.W. MERRICK sits clutching a coil of punched telegraph tape as if it were a holy relic. SHERIFF SETH BULLOCK stands stiffly by the door, radiating quiet rage. SOL STAR sits reasonably in a chair. CALAMITY JANE leans against the wall, drinking from a dark bottle.

**MERRICK**
*(Breathless)*
I tell you, gentlemen, the implications are epochal! Blazanov taps a query upon his key—sending it over the wire to some vast, steam-driven Analytical Engine situated in the bowels of Boston—and the wire spits back prose! Not mere numbers, Al. Reasoned thought! Conjured from the very ether!

**SWEARENGEN**
*(Turning slowly)*
Reasoned thought my ash, Merrick. It is a parlor trick. Orchestrated by some sanctimonious Yankton corksoakers looking to manipulate the price of gold before it leaves the Black Hills.

**BULLOCK**
*(Jaw clenched)*
If this machine starts wiring back edicts on how the law is to be applied in this camp, or questioning the validity of a man's claim based on some phantom eastern bullshirt calculation, I will tear the telegraph poles down with my bare hands.

**SOL STAR**
Seth, pace yourself. It's a calculating tool. Blazanov says it ingests every book and ledger ever put to print. If it can predict the cost of freight from Omaha before the wagons even load, it provides an advantage. We'd be fools to ignore the odds it calculates.

**CALAMITY JANE**
*(Burps, wiping her mouth)*
Odds be forked, and the forking horse they rode in on! I seen the Russian's fingers bleeding, tapping out questions to this brass brain! Forking invisible thinking box! A man asks it how to cure the bloody flux, and the wire taps back a recipe for boiled bark and mercury! It's a spook in the wires, and I won't have it taking the measure of my soul!

**MERRICK**
But Jane, consider the elevation of our discourse! Though... I must confess a profound, existential terror. If this Engine can generate editorials of unimpeachable logic and celestial grammar in mere seconds, what becomes of the Deadwood Pioneer? What becomes of the human endeavor to report the truth?

**SWEARENGEN**
*(Pours a whiskey, walks to his desk)*
The truth, Merrick, is a commodity. And its value is dictated by the man holding the biggest forking knife when the sun goes down.

SWEARENGEN sits, eyeing the coiled telegraph tape.

**SWEARENGEN** *(CONT'D)*
This "monstrous oracle" of yours doesn't think. It reads the collective, babbling idiocy of every unwashed hooplehead that ever set a line of type, distills it into a palatable mash, and spits it back into your lap. You ask it a question, it wires back whatever advances the interests of the invisible benches operating the bellows back East.

**BULLOCK**
Then we shut it down. We bar Blazanov from receiving its transmissions. A camp can't be governed by an unaccountable phantom. When a man lies to my face, I can put him in a cell. Who do I hang when the telegraph lies?

**SWEARENGEN**
You don't hang it, Sheriff. You learn how it lies, and you place your bets accordingly. We use the forking thing. We ask it what the politicians in Dakota Territory are plotting next. If it's right, we fortify our positions and profit. If it's wrong, we use Merrick's paper to expose the machine as a fraud and drive its stock into the mud.

Al downs his whiskey and points a finger at Merrick.

**SWEARENGEN** *(CONT'D)*
Until this brass brain learns to cross the mud to the Gem and buy its own forking whiskey, it is just another tool to be wielded. Now, take that tape, Merrick, and ask the engine how much Yankton is paying the Pinkertons this month. And God help Blazanov if he misses a dot or a dash.
